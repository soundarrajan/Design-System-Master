Container line Maersk announces New Jersey 0.5% sulfur bunker production deal
1 hour ago

AP Moller-Maersk, owners of the largest shipping company in the world, announced a deal with New Jersey-based storage and pipeline operator PBF Logistics to produce and store 0.5% sulfur marine fuels on the US East Coast.

Maersk will source crude oil and PBF Logistics will process it at its CPI Operations terminal in New Jersey, Maersk said Thursday. The deal envisages annual production of about 1.25 million mt/year, representing about 10% of Maersk's annual fuel demand.

The product will also be made available to external customers through Maersk Oil Trading.

 Singapore gasoil front-month swap near 3-month high on firmer outlook
2 hours ago
Excess gasoil supplies being cleared from Asia, regional turnarounds and firmer demand in the months ahead are supporting the firmer outlook.

The front-month March Singapore gasoil swaps spotted a near three-month high Wednesday on the back of steady trading activities coupled with firmer expectations on the near term outlook in the physical market.

At the Asian close Wednesday, the Asian gasoil swap jumped $1.21/b to $77.46/b, marking the highest level since November 22 when it was assessed at $78.13/b, S&P Global Platts data showed. Excess gasoil supplies being cleared from Asia, regional turnarounds and firmer demand in the months ahead are supporting the firmer outlook.

Market participants are hopeful that Chinese gasoil demand, which has been going through a lull period over the Lunar New Year period, will pick up in March when demand from the agricultural sector as well as construction projects start to pick up pace.

Still, the FOB Singapore cash differential for 10 ppm sulfur gasoil cargoes eased back slightly by 3 cents/b, falling to minus 45 cents/b to the Mean of Platts Singapore Gasoil assessments at the Asian close Wednesday.

The bullish sentiment was also evident further down the curve with the gasoil Q2/Q3 spread rising 6 cents/b day on day to minus 35 cents/b. On cracks, the front-month March Singapore gasoil swaps against Brent swaps -- which measures the relative value of the product to crude oil -- rose 18 cents/b to be assessed at $14.51/b on Wednesday.

Meanwhile, the Q2 gasoil cracks against Brent averaged $14.79/b Wednesday, further supporting a firmer outlook in the near term Singapore gasoil market.

 Dominica-flagged supply vessel sinks in Singapore waters: MPA
3 hours ago
There was no oil pollution or injury reported, the MPA said.

 The Maritime and Port Authority of Singapore said that Dominica-flagged supply vessel Ocean Cooper 2 had sunk in Singapore Territorial Waters in the Singapore Strait at about 7:15 am Singapore time (1115 GMT) on Thursday.

The supply vessel capsized and sank in the westbound lane of the Traffic Separation Scheme, about 3 nautical miles from Pedra Branca, MPA said in a statement.

There was no oil pollution or injury reported, the MPA said, adding that traffic in the Singapore Strait remains unaffected.

All three Indonesian crew were rescued by a nearby accompanying supply vessel, Jolly Rachel, it said.

Meanwhile, MPA has also issued navigational broadcasts asking ships to navigate with caution in the vicinity of the incident and has deployed a craft to conduct a hydrographic survey of the wreck, it said.

MPA is still investigating the incident, it said.

Singapore is the world’s largest bunkering port. Total arrivals of vessels with over 75 gross tonnage in the Port of Singapore stood at 140,768 in 2018.
